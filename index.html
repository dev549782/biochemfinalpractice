What is the MCAT?

The MCAT is a standardized exam required for admission to most medical schools in the United States and Canada. It assesses your knowledge in Biological and Biochemical Foundations, Chemical and Physical Foundations, Psychological, Social, and Biological Foundations, and Critical Analysis & Reasoning Skills.

Exam Structure

Biological & Biochemical Foundations

95 questions | 95 minutes

Chemical & Physical Foundations

95 questions | 95 minutes

Psychological, Social, & Biological Foundations

95 questions | 95 minutes

Critical Analysis & Reasoning Skills

53 questions | 90 minutes

Top Study Tips

  • Begin your preparation 6+ months in advance.
  • Create a balanced study schedule covering all sections equally.
  • Use active learning: flashcards, practice passages, and teaching concepts.
  • Regularly take full-length practice exams under timed conditions.
  • Review your mistakes thoroughly to identify and address weak areas.
